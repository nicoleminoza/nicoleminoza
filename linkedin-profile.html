About

I own both the product and the story that sells it.

For more than a decade, I led product strategy and marketing for Adobe's fonts and typography business, a service everyone used but no one measured. I made the case that typography drives business, then proved it: conversion, retention, and platform growth, with the numbers to defend every claim.

What I do:

  • Platform strategy at scale. I led product strategy and marketing for a 75-person cross-functional organization inside Adobe's $17.65B Digital Media segment. I grew Adobe Fonts to 12M+ monthly active users (MAU) and unified fragmented per-app text engines into one platform now serving 15M MAU across 12 flagship apps. Every integration was secured by persuading teams I didn't manage.
  • Product marketing that moves revenue. Redesigned font recommendations in Adobe Express, Adobe's freemium design app, lifting free-to-paid conversion +186%; led the rebrand of Typekit, an acquired web-font service, into Adobe Fonts to +40% MAU in year one with zero losses among the type foundries that license fonts to Adobe.
  • Business model and partnerships. Shipped a usage-based royalty model that saved $1M in year-one royalties; launched Adobe's largest licensing partnership in five years (Monotype and Microsoft), 1.2M users in weeks.
  • AI, hands-on. Defined Adobe's AI product strategy for fonts and typography and shipped generative text edit in Firefly Boards, Adobe's AI-powered infinite canvas, at Adobe MAX, Adobe's flagship creativity conference. Independently, I built and shipped Ramón, an AI styling app, end-to-end on the Claude API, from product requirements to UX copy to server-side security.

I lead at the intersection of product, marketing, and AI, and leverage the same playbooks whether the work is a platform, a creative or developer tool, or an AI-native product: start with what a technology is for, then build the system and the narrative to get it there.
