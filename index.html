04

How I think

The examples are fonts; the playbooks carry to any product.

Playbook 01Influence

Adoption without a mandate

You don't mandate a platform onto teams you don't manage. You make integrating it their win, and you measure the number they respect.

Result One font platform now powers 12 flagship apps and 15M users, built entirely by persuading teams I didn't manage.
  1. Make the integration the app team's win, not yours.
  2. Start where you're wanted: flagship anchors first, then the smaller apps.
  3. Standardize that component so each new app costs less to ship.
  4. Measure usage, not installs: report unique users actually using the feature.
Playbook 02Measurement

Unimpeachable by design

A number is only as strong as where it came from. Provenance, not precision, is what lets a result survive an executive room.

  1. Define success metrics with the data team before launch. Adoption, failed-search rate, missing-font events.
  2. Build the dashboards pre-launch so impact is read, not reconstructed.
  3. Tie each metric back to the original business-case claim: drop-off pattern → search failure → adoption.
Result+186% free-to-paid conversion the day Adobe Express shipped, instrumented from day one.
Playbook 03Positioning

Two buyers, one capability

Same capability, two true positions. Never average two audiences into one generic message.

  1. Individual designers → led with creative freedom (Bring Your Own Fonts).
  2. Enterprise teams → led with governance and control (Custom Fonts).
  3. Both shipped under their own name; neither averaged into the other.
Decision ruleOne capability can carry two messages; it must never carry an average of them.
