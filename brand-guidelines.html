01

Foundation

The brand reads like a well-set page, not a tech deck. It is evidence-led, typographically literate, and quietly confident: a serif voice for judgment, a clean sans for clarity, and a mono for the receipts. Every choice should feel considered, measured, and earned.

01

Evidence over adjectives

Numbers carry the argument. The design gives data room to breathe rather than dressing it up.

02

Editorial, not corporate

Magazine logic: strong type, generous rules, real hierarchy. No gradient soup, no stock gloss.

03

Craft is the message

A career built on typography should look it. Restraint and precision are the brand's proof of work.

Do

  • Keep the terracotta period; it’s the one piece of color in the mark.
  • Give the wordmark clear space of at least the cap-height on every side.
  • Use the “N” glyph only where the full name won’t fit (favicons, avatars).
  • Set it in Newsreader 600; never substitute another serif.

Don’t

  • Recolor the wordmark in terracotta or crimson; use ink, paper, or plum only.
  • Add shadows, outlines, or gradients to the mark.
  • Stretch, condense, or re-letter-space the name.
  • Place the mark on a busy photo without an ink or paper backing.

05

Voice & tone

Confident without bravado. Specific without jargon. Every claim is one a real person would defend in a real room, and backs it with a number.

We sound like

  • Plainspoken and direct: short declaratives, active verbs.
  • Evidence-led: the metric does the boasting.
  • Calm authority: “calls I’d defend in any room.”
  • Literate: typographically aware, never precious.

We never sound like

  • Hype or superlatives without proof (“world-class,” “rockstar”).
  • Corporate filler (“synergy,” “leverage learnings”).
  • Emoji, exclamation stacks, or breathless launch-speak.
  • Vague impact with no number behind it.
Before · vague

“Drove incredible growth and transformed the typography business with a world-class strategy.”

After · on-brand

Grew Adobe Fonts to 12M+ monthly users, outpacing Creative Cloud 3×, on influence, across 12 apps I didn’t own.

Components

The small set of parts every page is built from. Pill buttons, mono labels, hairline-ruled cards, and accent-barred metrics.

Buttons

Fully-rounded pills. Solid plum for primary; ghost with a hairline border for secondary. Hover settles to ink and lifts 1px.

Labels & nav pill

Mono, uppercase, wide-tracked. The terracotta eyebrow label tags sections; the plum pill is the single nav CTA.
